# Flight Deck & Scholar Intro Video Scripts
## Two Videos — One Per Mode, Embedded on Site

**Voice:** Confident, authoritative narrator — David Attenborough-inspired gravitas with warmth
**Tone:** Inspiring mission briefing — you are being trusted with something important
**Language:** Sophisticated but accessible, no academic jargon, vivid metaphors
**Duration:** ~25-30 seconds each (4 clips per video)

---

## VIDEO 4: FLIGHT DECK — "Your Cockpit. Your Mission."

### Clip 1 (7s) — THE HOOK
**Narration:** "Twelve thousand years of civilisation. Ninety-one inventions that changed everything. Five great webs connecting them all. This is your cockpit."
**Visual:** Camera sweeps across a vast star field, then dives into a glowing holographic HUD cockpit. The Dearden Field grid materialises — 60 nodes pulsing with light across 12 columns and 5 rows. Cyan and gold energy lines connect them.

### Clip 2 (7s) — THE FLEET
**Narration:** "Choose your craft. Pathfinder, Forgemaster, Starcaster, Lifeline, or Sentinel — each one built for a different kind of mind."
**Visual:** Five spacecraft silhouettes emerge from a hangar bay, each glowing a different colour — green, amber, blue, pink, gold. They fan out into formation. HUD targeting brackets lock onto each one with its name and class designation.

### Clip 3 (7s) — THE MISSION
**Narration:** "Navigate the Dearden Field. Unlock relays. Build your ability scores. Every mission you complete pushes the clock further from midnight."
**Visual:** The cockpit HUD activates — a relay node lights up, the craft accelerates toward it, energy streams past. The node unlocks with a burst of golden light. An ability score meter rises. The Civilisation Clock's hands move backward.

### Clip 4 (5s) — THE CTA
**Narration:** "Commander — your craft is waiting. Take the controls."
**Visual:** First-person cockpit view. Hands reach for the flight controls. The Dearden Field stretches out ahead, glowing with possibility. "FLIGHT DECK" title blazes across the screen in military stencil font. Engine ignition flare.

**Total narration: ~26 seconds**

---

## VIDEO 5: SCHOLAR — "Write the Thesis That Changes Everything"

### Clip 1 (7s) — THE HOOK
**Narration:** "Every civilisation that ever fell had one thing in common — they forgot what built them. You are here to make sure that never happens again."
**Visual:** Ancient library crumbling — scrolls falling, columns breaking. Then time reverses: the library rebuilds itself, books fly back to shelves, light floods in. A modern scholar's desk appears with a glowing manuscript.

### Clip 2 (7s) — THE FRAMEWORK
**Narration:** "Create your character. Roll your abilities. Choose your FITS temperament. Then begin the deepest journey of all — understanding why infrastructure matters."
**Visual:** A character creation screen materialises — a d20 dice rolls across a star map, ability scores light up one by one. A FITS compass spins and locks into position. The four pillars — Observe, Educate, Apply, Thesis — rise like columns of light.

### Clip 3 (7s) — THE THESIS
**Narration:** "Twelve chapters. Twelve relays. One thesis that connects fire to the internet, roads to rockets, and you to twelve thousand years of human ingenuity."
**Visual:** A glowing manuscript page fills with text — chapter headings appear one by one. Golden threads connect them across a timeline. The 12 relay icons orbit the manuscript like planets around a sun. The thesis title illuminates.

### Clip 4 (5s) — THE CTA
**Narration:** "The line is not safe until the system is tested. Begin your thesis."
**Visual:** A pen touches parchment. Golden ink flows. The words "SCHOLAR MODE" appear in elegant serif. The Civilisation Clock's hands sweep backward. A golden seal stamps the page: APPROVED.

**Total narration: ~26 seconds**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Flight Deck audience (14-18):**
- Responds to: mission briefings, cockpit immersion, fleet selection, ability progression
- Understands: HUD interfaces, stat systems, strategic choice, team roles
- Key hook: "This is YOUR cockpit" — ownership and responsibility

**Scholar audience (18+):**
- Responds to: intellectual challenge, thesis writing, historical depth, academic achievement
- Understands: character creation, ability scores, four-pillar frameworks, academic grading
- Key hook: "Every civilisation that fell forgot what built them" — stakes and purpose

**The Blip translated for these audiences:**
- Flight Deck: "Every mission pushes the clock further from midnight" (action-oriented)
- Scholar: "You are here to make sure that never happens again" (purpose-oriented)

**BGM:** Same anime adventure track as Explorer videos for brand consistency, or a slightly more cinematic variant for the older audience.
