## 3. THE HUMAN BLIP

> "On the 4.5-billion-year Planetary Clock, all of human civilisation — every city, every empire, every road, every bridge, every satellite — occurs in the last fraction of a second before midnight."

The Human Blip is the ratio of human civilisation to Earth's history, expressed as a single number:

**β = 12,000 ÷ 4,500,000,000 = 2.667 × 10⁻⁶ = 0.000267%**

## 4. THE SURVIVAL FORMULA

> "If human civilisation is just 0.000267% of Earth's history, what accuracy and precision do we need to survive? The answer is a formula — the civilisational equivalent of E = mc²."

### 4.1 The Equation

$$S = \frac{A \times P}{\beta}$$

| Symbol | Name | Range | Definition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **S** | Survival Index | dimensionless | The civilisational survival metric |
| **A** | Accuracy | 0 → 1 | Are we aimed at the right target? Right direction, right knowledge, right understanding |
| **P** | Precision | 0 → 1 | Are we consistently hitting it? Repeatable, reliable, transmissible across generations |
| **β** | Human Blip Factor | 2.667 × 10⁻⁶ | The fragility constant — 12,000 years of 4.5 billion |

### 4.2 The Tipping Point

The survival threshold is reached when S equals or exceeds the inverse of the Human Blip Factor:

**S ≥ β⁻¹ = 375,000**

This means the collective product of accuracy and precision across the network must overcome a **375,000× fragility multiplier**. Every 1% error in direction or consistency is amplified 375,000 times by the sheer brevity of human existence on the planetary timescale.

## 5. HARRISON'S 4-GRID — SURVIVAL PROBABILITY

The 4-Grid maps the four possible combinations of high and low accuracy and precision against the fragility multiplier to produce survival probabilities. It is named after John Harrison, the clockmaker whose precision instruments solved the longitude problem — a direct precedent for the relationship between accuracy, precision, and civilisational navigation.

| Scenario | Accuracy | Precision | A×P | Error × β⁻¹ | Survival Probability |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **BULLSEYE** (High A + High P) | 0.95 | 0.95 | 0.9025 | 36,563× | **90.25%** |
| **SCATTERED** (High A + Low P) | 0.95 | 0.40 | 0.3800 | 232,500× | **38.00%** |
| **WRONG AIM** (Low A + High P) | 0.30 | 0.95 | 0.2850 | 268,125× | **28.50%** |
| **CHAOS** (Low A + Low P) | 0.20 | 0.20 | 0.0400 | 360,000× | **4.00%** |

The grid demonstrates that precision without accuracy (Wrong Aim) is nearly as dangerous as total chaos. High accuracy without precision (Scattered) halves survival probability. Only the Bullseye — high accuracy combined with high precision — produces a viable survival outcome. The IAAI provides the gyroscope (accuracy through correct knowledge) and the methodology (precision through repeatable, transmissible education).
