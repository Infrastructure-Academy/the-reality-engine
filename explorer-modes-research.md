# Explorer Mode Research — Three Game Mechanic Archetypes

## 1. MOLDVAY BASIC D&D (1981) — "The Red Box"

### Why It Worked for Young Players (Ages 10+)
- **14-step character creation** — simple, sequential, fits on an index card
- **3d6 ability scores** rolled in order — random generation creates unique characters every time
- **Low modifier impact** — scores 6-15 give only +/-1, so "bad rolls" don't cripple you
- **Race-as-class** — Dwarf, Elf, Halfling ARE the class (no separate race + class decision)
- **7 classes total** — Cleric, Fighter, Magic-User, Thief, Dwarf, Elf, Halfling
- **Dungeon crawl structure** — explore room by room, encounter monsters, find treasure
- **Mapper and Caller roles** — collaborative play, not just individual
- **"For 3 or More Adults, Ages 10 and Up"** — explicitly designed for beginners
- **Compact rules** — entire Basic set is ~64 pages, covers levels 1-3
- **Expert set** extends to levels 4-14 with wilderness adventures

### Key Mechanics to Adapt
- Random character generation (d20 rolls — already in Scholar mode)
- Room-by-room dungeon exploration (relay-by-relay)
- Encounter tables (random invention discoveries)
- Treasure/XP per room (BitPoints per relay)
- Simple combat (pass/fail checks, not complex)
- Party roles (mapper = navigator, caller = leader)
- Levels 1-3 only (keep scope tight)

## 2. ONE-ARMED BANDIT / SLOT MACHINE MECHANICS

### Psychology — Variable Ratio Reinforcement
- **Variable ratio schedule** — reward after unpredictable number of responses
- **Most resistant to extinction** — players keep going longest under VR schedules
- **Near-miss effect** — almost winning feels like progress, drives continued play
- **Dopamine release** — anticipation of reward, not reward itself, drives engagement
- **Small frequent wins + rare big wins** — maintains engagement across sessions

### Ethical Adaptation for Education (8-14)
- **NOT gambling** — no money, no real loss, always progress
- **Spin-to-discover** — each spin reveals a relay invention (always a win, but rarity varies)
- **Common / Uncommon / Rare / Legendary** — rarity tiers for discoveries
- **Streak bonuses** — consecutive plays increase chances of rare finds
- **Collection completion** — the drive to "get them all" (Pokémon effect)
- **Multi-armed bandit** — choose WHICH relay to spin (strategic choice)
- **Guaranteed minimum** — every spin gives something (XP, lore, or invention)

### Key Mechanics to Adapt
- 3-reel spinner with relay symbols (Fire/Tree/River etc.)
- Match 3 = discovery unlock
- Match 2 = partial reveal / lore fragment
- No match = XP consolation prize
- Daily spins (engagement loop)
- Bonus rounds for relay completion
- Progressive jackpot = rare cross-relay discovery

## 3. HE-MAN / MASTERS OF THE UNIVERSE / CASTLE GRAYSKULL

### Why It Worked for Kids (1983-1987)
- **Transformation mechanic** — "BY THE POWER OF GRAYSKULL!" — weak becomes strong
- **Binary power state** — Prince Adam (normal) → He-Man (powered up)
- **Castle as power source** — physical location = power hub
- **Clear good vs evil** — He-Man vs Skeletor, no moral ambiguity
- **Toy-driven narrative** — each character = collectible with unique power
- **13 levels** in the GBA game (Power of Grayskull) — mission-based progression
- **Power-ups through missions** — earn abilities by completing challenges
- **Iconic catchphrase** — ritual transformation creates emotional moment

### Key Mechanics to Adapt
- **Transformation ritual** — complete a relay = power up (visual transformation)
- **Castle Grayskull = The Dearden Field** — the power source, the matrix
- **Prince Adam → He-Man** = Explorer → Relay Master (visual upgrade)
- **Each relay = a unique power** — Fire gives flame shield, River gives flow mastery, etc.
- **Skeletor = The Clock Approaching Midnight** — the antagonist is civilisational fragility
- **Power Sword = The Survival Formula** — S = (A×P)/β is the weapon
- **Collectible characters** — each relay has a guardian character (like MOTU figures)
- **"I HAVE THE POWER!"** moment — completing all 12 relays = full transformation

### Visual Style
- Bold, muscular, primary colours
- Castle with skull motif (could be clock face)
- Transformation sequences with lightning/energy
- Character cards with stats (like toy packaging)
- Power levels displayed as bars/meters

| Mode | Best Age | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Slot Spinner | 8-10 | Simple tap, instant reward, visual, no reading required |
| D&D Dungeon Crawl | 10-12 | Some reading, strategic choices, party play |
| Grayskull Power-Up | 12-14 | Narrative, transformation, character identity |
| Current Explorer | 8-14 | Tap-to-discover, works but one-size-fits-all |
