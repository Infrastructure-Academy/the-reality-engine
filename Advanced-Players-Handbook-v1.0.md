## 1. THE FLIGHT DECK

### 1.1 What Is the Flight Deck?

The Flight Deck is the cockpit of civilisational navigation. It is designed for players aged 14–18 who are ready for a more immersive, strategic experience. When you enter the Flight Deck, you are no longer a passive explorer — you are a pilot. Your craft has stats. Your missions have objectives. The Dearden Field stretches out before you like a star chart, and every node you activate brings you closer to understanding how the relays and webs interconnect.

The Flight Deck has two phases. The first is **Craft Selection**, where you choose your Thunderbird-class vessel based on your FITS temperament alignment. The second is the **HUD** (Heads-Up Display), where you navigate the Dearden Field, activate nodes, and complete missions with DAVID as your co-pilot.

### 1.2 Entering the Flight Deck

To access the Flight Deck, navigate to the home page and select the **Flight Deck** mode card, or use the bottom navigation bar on mobile. The Flight Deck requires authentication — you must be signed in to save your progress, track node activations, and maintain your XP balance.

When you first enter, you will see the Craft Selection screen. This is where you choose your vessel for the journey ahead.

The craft selection screen presents all five vessels with their concept art, stats, role description, and FITS alignment. You can review each craft before committing. Node activations are permanent regardless of which craft you used to activate them.

If you are fascinated by communication and connection, choose TB-3. If you care about people and healing, choose TB-4. If you see the big picture and want to coordinate everything, choose TB-5.

## 3. THE DEARDEN FIELD

### 3.1 What Is the Dearden Field?

The Dearden Field is the 60-node matrix that forms the core navigation system of Flight Deck mode. It is constructed by intersecting the **12 Civilisational Relays** (rows) with the **5 Great Webs** (columns), producing a 12 x 5 grid of 60 unique nodes.

Each node represents a specific intersection of a relay and a web. For example:

- **Fire x Natural** — How controlled fire shaped ecosystems and early agriculture
- **Loom x Digital** — How weaving patterns led to binary code and programmable machines
- **Rail x Machine** — How standardised gauge transformed continental logistics
- **Human Nodes x Consciousness** — The frontier of collective intelligence and AI

The Dearden Field is not just a navigation tool — it is a **knowledge map**. When you activate a node, you are not simply ticking a box. You are exploring a genuine intersection of history, technology, and civilisation. The connections between nodes reveal patterns that are invisible in the linear relay sequence.

### 3.2 The HUD

Once you select your craft, the Flight Deck transitions to the HUD view. The HUD displays:

- **The Dearden Field matrix** — a 12 x 5 grid of interactive nodes, colour-coded by web
- **Your craft identity** — shown in the header with your pilot designation
- **Node activation count** — your progress toward 60/60 completion
- **XP counter** — your running total, updated in real time as you activate nodes
- **DAVID co-pilot** — accessible via the chat panel for guidance and mission briefings

Activated nodes glow with their web colour. Unactivated nodes appear dimmed. You can pinch-to-zoom on the matrix for a closer view, and long-press any node to see a tooltip with its relay, web, and description.

### 3.3 Activating Nodes

To activate a node, tap it on the Dearden Field. Each activation awards XP based on the relay and web, with your craft's bonus applied if applicable. DAVID provides contextual narration for each activation, explaining the historical significance of that particular relay-web intersection.

When you reach 60/60 — every node activated — a confetti celebration triggers and you earn the **Dearden Field Complete** achievement. This is one of the most significant milestones in the entire Reality Engine.

### 3.4 Gamepad Support

The Flight Deck supports gamepad navigation for console-style play. Use the D-pad to move between nodes, the A button to activate, and the bumpers to switch between the matrix and the chat panel.

## 4. FITS TEMPERAMENT SYSTEM

### 4.1 What Is FITS?

FITS stands for **Feeler, Intuitive, Thinker, Senser** — the four civilisational temperament types that describe how you naturally approach infrastructure and knowledge. In Explorer mode, DAVID observes your choices silently and builds a profile. In Scholar mode, you take a formal 6-question assessment that reveals your type explicitly.

The FITS system is not a personality test in the pop-psychology sense. It is a framework for understanding how different civilisational perspectives — Western (analytical), Eastern (holistic), Nomadic (adaptive), and Humanitarian (empathetic) — manifest in individual learners.

### 4.2 The Four Types

**Senser (S)** — You are drawn to the tangible, the observable, the physical. You notice materials, terrain, and environmental conditions before anyone else. In civilisational terms, you align with the **Natural Web** — fire, trees, rivers, the elemental forces that preceded all human engineering. Your craft is **TB-1 Pathfinder**, the reconnaissance vessel that arrives first and maps the unknown.

**Thinker (T)** — You are drawn to structure, logic, and systematic analysis. You see infrastructure as a series of engineering problems to be solved. In civilisational terms, you align with the **Machine Web** — horses, roads, ships, rail, engines, the physical systems that moved people and goods. Your craft is **TB-2 Forgemaster**, the heavy engineering platform that builds civilisation wherever it lands.

**Intuitive (I)** — You are drawn to connections, patterns, and paradigm shifts. You see the invisible threads that link one relay to another. In civilisational terms, you align with the **Digital Web** — looms, communications, computing, the signal networks that connected the world. Your craft is **TB-3 Starcaster**, the communications vessel that weaves the web across all nodes.

**Feeler (F)** — You are drawn to people, stories, and human impact. You care about who was helped and who was harmed by each civilisational advance. In civilisational terms, you align with the **Biological Web** — medicine, genetics, biological engineering, the systems that sustain human life. Your craft is **TB-4 Lifeline**, the medical vessel that carries the conscience of the fleet.

**Balanced (B)** — You score evenly across all four dimensions. You do not specialise — you coordinate. In civilisational terms, you align with the **Consciousness Web** — awareness, philosophy, collective intelligence, the frontier that lies beyond all physical infrastructure. Your craft is **TB-5 Sentinel**, the command vessel that holds the Dearden Field together from above.

### 4.3 The Assessment

In Scholar mode, the FITS assessment consists of 6 questions, each with 4 answer options (one per type). Your answers are tallied, and the type with the highest score becomes your primary FITS designation. If two or more types are tied, you are classified as **Balanced**.

The assessment is not pass/fail. There are no wrong answers. Every type is equally valuable — civilisation needed all four perspectives to reach where it is today, and it will need all four to survive what comes next.

### 4.4 Developmental Progression

The FITS system is designed as a developmental framework, not a fixed label. Players are encouraged to develop all four aspects over time, aiming for mastery across all dimensions. Your initial assessment reveals your natural starting point; your journey through the Dearden Field and thesis work develops the others.

## 5. THE SIX ABILITY SCORES

### 5.1 Overview

The Scholar and Flight Deck tiers use a full six-ability-score system, expanding the three simplified scores from Explorer mode (OBS, INT, RES) into a more nuanced framework.

| Ability | Abbreviation | Domain |
|---|---|---|
| **Observation** | OBS | Perception and awareness of infrastructure patterns |
| **Analysis** | ANA | Logical reasoning and systems thinking |
| **Synthesis** | SYN | Combining knowledge across domains |
| **Communication** | COM | Articulating ideas and persuading others |
| **Implementation** | IMP | Practical application and project execution |
| **Vision** | VIS | Strategic foresight and civilisational thinking |

### 5.2 Rolling Your Scores

In Scholar mode, ability scores are rolled using the **4d6 drop lowest** method — roll four six-sided dice, discard the lowest, and sum the remaining three. This produces a range of 3–18 with a bell curve centred around 12–13. You get three attempts to roll a complete set; you keep whichever set you prefer.

After rolling all six abilities, you make a single **D20 bonus roll**. This roll determines your starting XP bonus:

- **Starting XP** = (Sum of all ability scores x 10,000) + (D20 result x 5,000)
- A **Natural 20** on the D20 is a Critical Success, awarding the maximum 100,000 XP bonus

### 5.3 Modifiers

Each ability score produces a modifier using the standard formula: **Modifier = floor((Score - 10) / 2)**. Modifiers range from -4 (score 3) to +4 (score 18) and influence mission outcomes, thesis grading, and Dearden Field navigation.

| Score Range | Modifier | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 3–5 | -3 to -4 | Significant disadvantage |
| 6–9 | -1 to -2 | Slight disadvantage |
| 10–11 | 0 | Average |
| 12–15 | +1 to +2 | Slight advantage |
| 16–18 | +3 to +4 | Significant advantage |

### 5.4 How Abilities Affect Gameplay

Each ability score influences different aspects of the Flight Deck and Scholar experience:

**Observation (OBS)** affects your ability to detect hidden nodes on the Dearden Field and notice connections between relays that other players miss. High OBS means more bonus content revealed during node activations.

**Analysis (ANA)** affects your performance on analytical missions and thesis methodology. High ANA means stronger logical arguments in your thesis and better outcomes on systems-thinking challenges.

**Synthesis (SYN)** affects your ability to combine knowledge from multiple relays into coherent frameworks. High SYN means richer cross-relay connections in your thesis and bonus XP for multi-web node activations.

**Communication (COM)** affects how effectively DAVID can relay your ideas and how persuasive your thesis arguments are. High COM means better academic grades on the communication dimension.

**Implementation (IMP)** affects practical application challenges — can you take what you have learned and apply it? High IMP means bonus XP on application-focused missions and stronger practical sections in your thesis.

**Vision (VIS)** affects strategic foresight challenges and your ability to see the big picture. High VIS means access to advanced Dearden Field patterns and stronger thesis conclusions about civilisational futures.

## 6. SCHOLAR MODE

### 6.1 What Is Scholar Mode?

Scholar mode is the complete Infrastructure Odyssey experience, designed for players aged 18 and above. It combines the Flight Deck's navigation system with a full academic framework — character creation, FITS assessment, ability score rolling, thesis development, and academic grading. Scholar mode requires authentication.

### 6.2 The Four Phases

Scholar mode progresses through four phases:

**Phase 1: FITS Assessment** — Answer 6 questions to determine your civilisational temperament. Your FITS type is revealed at the end, along with your score breakdown across all four dimensions.

**Phase 2: Ability Score Rolling** — Roll your six ability scores using 4d6 drop lowest, plus a D20 bonus roll. You have three attempts to roll a set you are satisfied with. Your starting XP is calculated from these rolls.

**Phase 3: Character Sheet** — Review your complete character: name, FITS type, all six ability scores with modifiers, D20 bonus, starting XP, and your position on the 24,000,000 XP journey to Odyssey Complete.

**Phase 4: Thesis Development** — The heart of Scholar mode. Here you define your thesis title, track your relay research progress, and work toward an academic grade.

### 6.3 DAVID as Dungeon Master

In Scholar mode, DAVID shifts from co-pilot to **Dungeon Master**. He does not simply narrate — he challenges, questions, and guides. When you activate a Dearden Field node, DAVID asks you to articulate what you have learned. When you add a relay to your thesis, DAVID probes your reasoning. The interaction is Socratic — DAVID's role is to make you think harder, not to give you answers.

DAVID is accessible via the chat panel throughout all four phases. You can ask him questions about any relay, web, invention, or framework. He responds in a formal, analytical register appropriate to the academic context.

## 7. THESIS WRITING

### 7.1 The Thesis Framework

Your thesis is the culmination of the Scholar journey. It is structured around the 12 Civilisational Relays — each relay you research adds depth and breadth to your argument. The thesis title is yours to choose, but it must relate to infrastructure and civilisation.

Example thesis titles:

- "The Role of Water Infrastructure in Civilisational Emergence"
- "From Fire to Fibre: How Energy Relays Shaped the Modern World"
- "The Loom as Proto-Computer: Binary Thinking Before Babbage"
- "Roads, Rails, and Routes: The Machine Web's Contribution to Globalisation"
- "Human Nodes: Can Collective Intelligence Prevent Civilisational Collapse?"

### 7.2 Relay Research

To build your thesis, you must research relays by completing them on the Dearden Field. Each relay you complete adds to your thesis progress. The thesis tracker shows a grid of all 12 relays — completed relays are highlighted, incomplete relays are dimmed.

You do not need to complete all 12 relays to submit a thesis, but your academic grade depends heavily on how many you have researched. A thesis grounded in 12 relays will always outperform one grounded in 6.

### 7.3 The 4-Pillar Framework

Your thesis follows the **4-Pillar Framework** from The Guide (Part 2 of An Infrastructure Odyssey):

| Pillar | Purpose | What DAVID Looks For |
|---|---|---|
| **Observational** | What did you see? What patterns emerged? | Evidence of genuine engagement with relay content |
| **Educational** | What did you learn? What surprised you? | Demonstration of new knowledge acquisition |
| **Application** | How does this apply to the real world? | Practical connections between historical infrastructure and modern challenges |
| **Thesis** | What is your argument? What do you conclude? | A coherent, evidence-based argument about infrastructure and civilisation |

### 7.4 Extra Credits

Extra credits are awarded for time spent in the game beyond the minimum thesis requirements. They reflect dedication to learning, process, and strategy implementation. Extra credits contribute to your XP total and can push a borderline grade upward.

## 8. ACADEMIC ASSESSMENT

### 8.1 Grading Thresholds

Scholar mode uses a UK-style academic grading system with four tiers:

| Grade | Classification | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Distinction** | First Class | 12 relays researched + 20,000,000 XP |
| **Merit** | Upper Second (2:1) | 10 relays researched + 15,000,000 XP |
| **Pass** | Lower Second (2:2) | 8 relays researched + 10,000,000 XP |
| **Third** | Third Class | 6 relays researched + 5,000,000 XP |

Below 6 relays and 5,000,000 XP, the thesis is classified as **In Progress** — not failed, because The Reality Engine has no failure states. You simply have more work to do.

### 8.2 How Grades Are Calculated

Your grade is determined by two factors: **relay completion count** and **total XP**. Both thresholds must be met for a given grade. A player with 12 relays but only 10,000,000 XP earns a Pass, not a Distinction. A player with 20,000,000 XP but only 8 relays also earns a Pass. Both dimensions matter.

The grading system is transparent — the thresholds are visible on the thesis tracker at all times. There are no hidden criteria, no subjective judgements, and no surprises. You always know exactly where you stand and what you need to do next.

### 8.3 The Role of the Professor DM

The automated grading system provides an estimated achievement level. In a formal educational context, the final grade is adjudicated by a human professor who reviews the thesis, the relay research, and the DAVID interaction logs. The system empowers the professor with data while leaving the final decision for official recognition in human hands.

### 8.4 The XP Journey to Odyssey Complete

The ultimate goal is **24,000,000 XP** — the hard cap that represents the theoretical maximum. Reaching this cap earns the title **Odyssey Complete**, the rarest achievement in The Reality Engine. The journey from starting XP (typically 70,000–180,000 depending on your rolls) to 24,000,000 is long, deliberate, and deeply rewarding.

| Milestone | XP Required | Title |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | 0 | Apprentice |
| First Gate | 100,000 | Relay Scout |
| Momentum | 500,000 | Relay Runner |
| Mastery | 1,000,000 | GURU |
| Elite | 5,000,000 | Grand Master |
| Cap | 24,000,000 | Odyssey Complete |

## 9. THE CLOCK AND THE MISSION

### 9.1 Why This Matters

Everything in The Reality Engine — the relays, the webs, the Dearden Field, the thesis, the XP — serves a single purpose: pushing the Civilisation Clock back from midnight. The clock represents the risk of civilisational collapse. The further it is from midnight, the safer we are. Every node you activate, every relay you research, every thesis argument you make contributes to the collective counterweight.

### 9.2 The 2,229 Seconds

The ISI framework (documented in TP-016) calculates that if 1 billion people each contribute **2.58% of their day** — that is 2,229 seconds, or 37 minutes and 9 seconds — the collective counterweight exceeds the civilisational risk. That is the mobilisation target. That is why The Reality Engine exists. Not to entertain, but to mobilise.

### 9.3 Your Part

As a Flight Deck pilot or Scholar, you are not just playing a game. You are part of a global effort to ensure that the knowledge of 12,000 years of infrastructure is not lost. Every session you play, every node you activate, every thesis paragraph you write moves the clock backward. The question is not whether you can make a difference. The question is whether you will.
